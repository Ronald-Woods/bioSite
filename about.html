About Keith

Keith O’Bryan grew up between Ypsilanti, Michigan and Mineola, Texas, which gave him a good mix of big-city and small-town life. That mix helped him learn how to get along with all kinds of people and stay grounded in his values. After high school, he went to MediaTech Institute, where he studied Audio Engineering and learned everything from live sound to studio work.

He spent 17 years working at Audio Electronics, fixing high-end audio gear. One of his proudest moments was helping build a one-of-a-kind recording console for a major studio in Austin—a project that got featured by the Museum of Magnetic Sound Recording.

When the shop closed in 2024, he shifted to legal media work, recording and streaming court hearings and depositions. He’s also been involved in the legal field, working as a mock trials, which he finds both challenging and interesting.

These days, he splits his time between his legal media job, running a home-based audio repair business, and raising his kids. He enjoys fishing, playing banjo and guitar, and working on motors in his free time. He’s also hoping to learn more about data analysis to open new doors in tech and law.

Friends would say he’s kind, hardworking, and always there when you need him. He stays motivated by a strong desire to keep learning, provide for his family, and avoid getting stuck in a rut.
